Georgia Oâ€™Keeffe was an American painter and one of the most significant artists of the 20th century. She was born in 1887 on a Wisconsin farm as the second of seven children. She was determined from a very young age to become an artist and studied multiple forms of painting and drawing from watercolor to charcoal. She studied at the Art Institute of Chicago and the Art Students League in New York, where she learned the techniques of traditional painting, but her own style continued to develop over the years as she studied, traveled, and was influenced by other artists. O'Keeffe created many forms of abstract art, including her famous close-ups of flowers, that many found to be very sensuous in nature. She is also known for her marriage to art dealer and photographer Alfred Stieglitz, who she lived with in New York. She was inspired by the Southwest landscape and relocated to New Mexico permanently after Stieglitz's death, where she lived until she died in 1986. Eleven years after her death, the Georgia O'Keeffe Museum was established in New Mexico. In 2014, one of her paintings sold for $44,405,000, which was said to have been more than three times the previous world auction record for any female artist at that time.
